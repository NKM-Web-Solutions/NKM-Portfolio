Case Study

Compassion Project

Social impact website that aims to educate visitors about systemic racism in the United States with the hopes of recognizing and preventing continuity of these harmful patterns.

Problem

The United States has a long history marked by instances of injustice against the black community. American capitalism itself was built upon the brutal foundations of black slavery. Despite commendable strides, the nation grapples with persistent challenges that demand a solemn reckoning.

Racial bias continues to manifest in tragic instances of police brutality against black individuals. Equally disconcerting is the glaring lack of mainstream media attention afforded to missing black children and women. Within the judicial realm, a disheartening reality unfolds, where black victims often find themselves devoid of the quality and care essential for the pursuit of justice.

Solution

Eliminate ignorance about the systemic racism in the United States. Make an impact by showing the alarming proximity in time of heinous acts committed against the black community. This was created in hopes to educate, inform and in turn disrupt the cyclical nature of history. Embracing the principle that "Those who forget history are doomed to repeat it," the intention is to build a future grounded in understanding, empathy, and positive change.

Style Guide

Color

The seriousness of the content and subject matter warranted use of stark contrast of black and white. Then an accent color was needed for emphasis that was bright and bold and a reddish orange was chosen to indicate passion, energy and action. The intent is to insinuate the danger that is never that far away and to invoke anger at the brutality against the black community for hundreds of years.
